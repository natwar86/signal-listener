## 1. 3PL / Outsourced Fulfillment

Companies that handle pick/pack/ship for ecommerce brands. These are Saltbox's **highest-signal competitors** -- their unhappy customers are the most likely to switch to co-warehousing.

### ShipBob

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **What they do** | Tech-enabled 3PL with 60+ fulfillment centers globally, offering 2-day shipping, inventory distribution, and a merchant dashboard for DTC brands. |
| **Target customer** | SMB to mid-market (300+ orders/month sweet spot) |
| **Shopify app** | Yes -- `shipbob` ([apps.shopify.com/shipbob](https://apps.shopify.com/shipbob)), 4.6/5 (274 reviews) |
| **Direct competitors** | ShipMonk, ShipHero, Flexport/Deliverr, Flowspace, Red Stag |
| **Review ratings** | Trustpilot 3.8/5 (963 reviews) -- 73% 5-star but 17% 1-star. Capterra 3.9/5. Highly polarized. |
| **Top complaints** | Hidden fees (15-30% shipping markup + 3% CC surcharge), slow support (48+ hrs, no phone), receiving delays, offboarding fees >$3,000, lost/mixed SKUs |
| **Why unhappy customers fit Saltbox** | Brands frustrated by hidden fees, loss of control over inventory, and inability to physically inspect/manage their products. Brands doing 300-2,000 orders/month who realize 3PL costs exceed self-fulfillment from a co-warehouse. Brands with custom/kitted products that 3PLs handle poorly. |

### ShipMonk

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **What they do** | 3PL specializing in subscription boxes, DTC, and small-to-mid brands with 12+ fulfillment centers across US, Canada, Mexico, UK, and Czech Republic. |
| **Target customer** | SMB (no order minimums) to mid-market |
| **Shopify app** | Yes -- `shipmonk` ([apps.shopify.com/shipmonk](https://apps.shopify.com/shipmonk)), 4.4/5 (96 reviews) |
| **Direct competitors** | ShipBob, ShipHero, Fulfillrite, Simpl Fulfillment |
| **Review ratings** | Shopify 3.1/5 (81 reviews). Trustpilot 70% 5-star but 20% 1-star. |
| **Top complaints** | Cross-shipped orders (wrong client's products sent), exit process takes 6+ months with continued billing, billing errors, hidden reconciliation fees, no FIFO picking, sloppy packing, inventory data frequently wrong |
| **Why unhappy customers fit Saltbox** | The 6+ month exit ordeal is notorious -- brands looking to leave ShipMonk need somewhere to put inventory *immediately*. Subscription box brands with complex kitting needs often realize they should do it themselves. Brands tired of cross-shipment errors want hands-on quality control. |

### ShipHero (Fulfillment side)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **What they do** | Dual offering: 3PL fulfillment services (7 US + 2 Canada warehouses) AND standalone WMS software ($1,995/mo). Claims 99.99% accuracy. Flat-rate zone-free shipping. |
| **Target customer** | Mid-market to enterprise (Shopify Plus users); WMS for brands doing self-fulfillment |
| **Shopify app** | Yes -- `shiphero` ([apps.shopify.com/shiphero](https://apps.shopify.com/shiphero)) |
| **Direct competitors** | ShipBob, ShipMonk (fulfillment); SKUSavvy, Cin7, Logiwa (WMS) |
| **Top complaints** | Higher price point, complex onboarding, WMS learning curve |
| **Why unhappy customers fit Saltbox** | ShipHero WMS users are **already self-fulfilling** -- they need warehouse space. These are ideal Saltbox customers who have the software but need the physical facility. ShipHero fulfillment customers leaving for cost reasons need a space to transition to self-fulfillment. |

### Fulfillrite

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|

| **Target customer** | SMB to mid-market, multi-channel sellers |

| **Direct competitors** | Shippo, Easyship, Pirate Ship, Veeqo |
| **Saltbox relevance** | **HIGH** -- ShipStation users are self-fulfilling and need warehouse space. These are the exact brands that benefit from co-warehousing. ShipStation's most expensive plans signal growing brands that may be outgrowing home/garage fulfillment. |

### Easyship

| Field | Detail |

| **What they do** | Shipping platform with strongest international focus -- duties/taxes at checkout, 250+ courier solutions, cross-border optimization. |

| **Direct competitors** | ShipStation, Shippo, Pirate Ship |
| **Saltbox relevance** | **HIGH** -- International sellers self-fulfilling with Easyship have complex logistics. They need physical space for staging, customs prep, and multi-carrier pickups that co-warehousing provides. |

## 4. WMS / Inventory Management

Users of standalone WMS often **self-fulfill** and need warehouse space. These are strong Saltbox signals because the brand has already invested in warehouse software -- they just need the physical space.

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|

| **Shopify app** | Yes -- `skusavvy` ([apps.shopify.com/skusavvy](https://apps.shopify.com/skusavvy)) |
| **Direct competitors** | ShipHero WMS, Cin7, Logiwa |
| **Saltbox relevance** | **VERY HIGH** -- SKUSavvy users are self-fulfilling by definition. They have invested in WMS software and need physical warehouse space. These brands are the ideal Saltbox customer profile. Already tracked in signal-listener. |

### ShipHero (WMS side)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|

## 7. Non-Shopify Platforms Where Frustrated Fulfillment Customers Congregate

These are **lead generation channels**, not competitors. They're where unhappy customers of the above companies go to vent and seek alternatives.

## Key Pain Points Driving Category Switching

These are the critical moments when brands move between fulfillment models. Each represents a **Saltbox acquisition opportunity**.

### Pain Point 1: Hidden 3PL Fees and Cost Escalation
- **Direction:** 3PL --> Self-fulfillment (co-warehouse)
- **Signal:** Brands discover 15-30% shipping markups, storage penalties (48.6% of warehouses now charge long-term storage fees, up from 23.3% prior year), receiving fees, credit card surcharges, and offboarding costs ($3,000+ at ShipBob)
- **Saltbox pitch:** "Know exactly what you're paying. Flat monthly rent, no per-order fees, no surprises."
- **Frequency:** Very common. Primary driver of 3PL churn.

### Pain Point 2: Loss of Quality Control
- **Direction:** 3PL --> Self-fulfillment (co-warehouse)
- **Signal:** Cross-shipped orders (ShipMonk), mixed SKUs, sloppy packing, no FIFO compliance, wrong items shipped. Error rates exceeding 2% with no corrective action plan.
- **Saltbox pitch:** "Your products, your hands, your quality standards. Walk into your suite and fulfill orders yourself."
- **Frequency:** Common. Brands with premium/custom products are especially sensitive.

### Pain Point 3: 3PL Exit Nightmares
- **Direction:** 3PL --> Any alternative (desperate)
- **Signal:** ShipMonk exit takes 6+ months with continued billing. ShipBob offboarding fees >$3,000. Brands literally trapped with inventory held hostage.
- **Saltbox pitch:** "Month-to-month. No exit fees. Your inventory is always yours."
- **Frequency:** Moderate but VERY high intent. Brands in exit mode are ready to sign today.

### Pain Point 4: Outgrowing Home/Garage Fulfillment
- **Direction:** Home fulfillment --> Co-warehouse
- **Signal:** Shipping software users (Pirate Ship, Shippo, ShipStation) hitting volume where home fulfillment is unsustainable. Orders >50/day from a garage. HOA complaints. Spouse complaints.
- **Saltbox pitch:** "Graduate from your garage to a professional space -- starting at 70 sq ft."
- **Frequency:** Very common. This is the primary Saltbox growth funnel.

### Pain Point 5: Amazon FBA Prep Crisis (January 2026)
- **Direction:** FBA-reliant sellers --> Need prep space
- **Signal:** Amazon ending all US prep/labeling services January 1, 2026. 82% of Amazon sellers use FBA. Thousands need to either outsource prep or do it themselves.
- **Saltbox pitch:** "Prep your FBA shipments in your own space, on your schedule. Carrier pickups included."
- **Frequency:** **MASSIVE, TIME-SENSITIVE OPPORTUNITY.** This is happening NOW (already in effect as of Jan 2026). Every Amazon seller who previously relied on Amazon's prep services needs a solution immediately.

### Pain Point 6: Custom/Kitted Products Don't Work with 3PLs
- **Direction:** 3PL --> Self-fulfillment (co-warehouse)
- **Signal:** Subscription box brands, custom-assembled products, products requiring special handling. ShipMonk "custom projects take months." 3PLs charge premium for kitting.
- **Saltbox pitch:** "Build your own kitting station. Assemble products exactly how you want."
- **Frequency:** Common for subscription boxes, custom bundles, and products with inserts.

### Pain Point 7: Scaling Beyond 3PL Price Thresholds
- **Direction:** 3PL --> Self-fulfillment (co-warehouse)
- **Signal:** Brands at 500-2,000 orders/month where 3PL per-order costs ($3-5/order + shipping markup) make self-fulfillment economically superior. The math flips around 1,000 orders/month for most brands.
- **Saltbox pitch:** "At your volume, self-fulfillment from a Saltbox suite saves $X/month vs. your 3PL."
- **Frequency:** Common. Brands doing the math realize co-warehousing + labor is cheaper than 3PL at moderate volumes.

### Pain Point 8: Wanting Physical Proximity to Inventory
- **Direction:** 3PL --> Self-fulfillment (co-warehouse)
- **Signal:** Brands wanting to inspect returns, manage inventory quality, photograph products, run pop-up events, or simply touch their products. Common with premium/luxury DTC brands.
- **Saltbox pitch:** "Be with your products. Use the creative studios for content. Ship from the same space."
- **Frequency:** Moderate. Stronger signal for premium brands and content-driven DTC businesses.

### Pain Point 9: Poor 3PL Technology / No Real-Time Visibility
- **Direction:** 3PL --> Self-fulfillment with own WMS
- **Signal:** Brands forced to email 3PL for inventory updates, no real-time dashboard, billing discrepancies. Customer service ratings as low as 1.3/5.
- **Saltbox pitch:** "Use your own WMS (SKUSavvy, ShipHero) and see every unit in real time."
- **Frequency:** Common. Drives adoption of standalone WMS, which then drives demand for warehouse space.

### Pain Point 10: Multi-Channel Complexity
- **Direction:** Single-channel fulfillment --> Need flexible space
- **Signal:** Brands selling on Shopify + Amazon + TikTok Shop + wholesale need to prep differently for each channel. 3PLs may not support all channels well.
- **Saltbox pitch:** "One space for all your channels. Prep FBA, ship DTC, and stage wholesale from the same suite."
- **Frequency:** Growing rapidly as social commerce expands (TikTok Shop, YouTube Shopping).
